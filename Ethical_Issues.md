# Ethical Issues

TeamMatch is designed to help instructors create balanced student teams based on skills, availability, and course requirements. Because the system uses student-provided information, ethical concerns include privacy, fairness, transparency, and possible misuse of student data.

One major ethical concern is student privacy. Students may provide sensitive information such as availability, skill level, confidence, and role preferences. The system should only collect information necessary for team formation and should not expose full student profiles to other students. Instructors should have access only for educational purposes.

Another ethical concern is fairness. If student skill levels are used incorrectly, students could be labeled unfairly as weak or strong contributors. To reduce this risk, TeamMatch should use skill data only for balanced team formation and not for grading or public ranking. The system should also avoid discrimination based on race, gender, religion, disability, or other protected factors.

TeamMatch could also be misused if an instructor or user manually manipulates team data unfairly. To address this, the system should provide transparent team explanations, maintain logs of changes, and allow instructors to review results before finalizing teams.

Overall, TeamMatch should prioritize fairness, transparency, and student privacy while supporting better collaboration in project-based courses.
